# Seasonal HVAC Maintenance Checklist for Florida Homeowners

In Pompano Beach, your HVAC system works nearly year-round to combat the heat and humidity. Unlike homeowners in cooler climates who give their AC a break during winter, South Florida residents depend on their cooling systems almost every day. That makes regular maintenance not just important -- it is essential. This checklist will help you stay on top of maintenance tasks throughout the year.

## Monthly Maintenance Tasks

These tasks should be performed every month regardless of the season:

**Check and Replace Air Filters:** In South Florida, air filters get dirty faster due to the constant operation of your system and the prevalence of pollen, dust, and humidity. Check your filter monthly and replace it every 30 to 60 days. A clogged filter restricts airflow, reduces efficiency, and can cause the evaporator coil to freeze.

**Inspect Vents and Registers:** Walk through your home and make sure all supply and return vents are open and unobstructed by furniture, curtains, or rugs. Blocked vents create pressure imbalances that make your system work harder.

**Check Thermostat Operation:** Verify that your thermostat is reading the correct temperature and the system responds appropriately when you adjust the settings. If your thermostat uses batteries, replace them annually.

**Clear the Condensate Drain Line:** Pour a quarter cup of white vinegar into the condensate drain line to prevent algae and mold buildup. Florida's humidity makes clogged drain lines one of the most common service calls in the Pompano Beach area.

## Spring Checklist (March through May)

Spring is the critical preparation period before the intense summer heat arrives:

- Schedule a professional tune-up with a licensed HVAC technician
- Clean or replace the air filter
- Clear debris, leaves, and vegetation from around the outdoor condenser unit (maintain at least two feet of clearance)
- Rinse the outdoor condenser coils with a garden hose to remove dirt and pollen buildup
- Test the system by running it in cooling mode for at least 30 minutes and checking that cold air comes from all vents
- Inspect the condensate drain pan for cracks or rust
- Check refrigerant lines for visible damage or oil stains that could indicate a leak
- Verify that the outdoor unit is level on its pad
- Test your carbon monoxide detector and smoke detectors

## Summer Checklist (June through September)

During the peak cooling season, vigilance is key:

- Replace air filters more frequently, every 30 days during heavy use
- Monitor your energy bills for unexpected increases that could signal a problem
- Keep blinds and curtains closed during peak sun hours to reduce the cooling load
- Ensure the thermostat is set no lower than 78 degrees when you are home to balance comfort and efficiency
- Check for unusual noises, odors, or performance changes
- Inspect weather stripping around doors and windows to prevent cool air from escaping
- Verify that ceiling fans are set to run counterclockwise to assist with cooling

## Fall Checklist (October through November)

Even as temperatures moderate slightly, your system still needs attention:

- Schedule a second professional tune-up
- Clean the outdoor condenser unit thoroughly
- Inspect and clean the evaporator coil and blower components
- Check ductwork for visible leaks, especially at joints and connections
- Test the heating function if your system provides heat (heat pump or electric heat strip)
- Seal any gaps around ductwork penetrations in walls and ceilings
- Consider having your ducts professionally cleaned if it has been more than three to five years

## Winter Checklist (December through February)

South Florida winters are mild, but your system still runs regularly:

- Continue monthly filter changes
- If temperatures drop below 60 degrees, test the heating mode to ensure it works properly
- Inspect the outdoor unit after any tropical storms or severe weather events
- Check the emergency drain pan under the indoor air handler for standing water
- Review your energy bills and compare them to the same period last year

## Professional Maintenance: What to Expect

When you schedule a professional tune-up, a qualified technician should perform the following:

- Check refrigerant levels and test for leaks
- Measure airflow across the evaporator coil
- Inspect and tighten all electrical connections
- Lubricate moving parts including fan motors and bearings
- Test the capacitor and contactor
- Evaluate overall system performance and efficiency
- Clean the evaporator and condenser coils
- Calibrate the thermostat
- Inspect the blower motor and wheel

## The Cost of Skipping Maintenance

Neglecting regular maintenance in the Pompano Beach climate leads to predictable consequences. Systems that are not maintained lose approximately five percent of their efficiency each year, meaning higher monthly bills. Lack of maintenance is also the leading cause of premature system failure, and most manufacturer warranties require proof of regular professional maintenance to remain valid.
