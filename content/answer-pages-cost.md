**Quick Answer:** Roof replacement costs typically range from $5,500 to $12,000 for an average 1,500-2,000 square foot home, with most homeowners paying around $8,500. If you're planning a AC installation, understanding the costs involved helps you budget appropriately and make informed decisions about materials and contractors.

## Average AC Installation Costs

The national average for complete AC installation is approximately $8,500, but costs vary significantly based on several factors.

### Cost by Home Size

- **1,000 sq ft home:** $4,500 - $7,500
- **1,500 sq ft home:** $6,500 - $9,500
- **2,000 sq ft home:** $7,500 - $12,000
- **2,500 sq ft home:** $9,500 - $15,000
- **3,000 sq ft home:** $11,500 - $18,000

These ranges assume standard asphalt shingle replacement on a home with average roof complexity.

### Cost by Roofing Square

Roofing professionals measure in "squares" (100 square feet). Here's what to expect:

- **Asphalt shingles:** $350 - $550 per square
- **Architectural shingles:** $400 - $650 per square
- **Metal roofing:** $600 - $1,200 per square
- **Tile roofing:** $700 - $1,500 per square
- **Slate roofing:** $1,000 - $3,000 per square

A typical home has 15-20 squares of roofing, making material choice a significant cost factor.

## AC Installation Cost by Material

Material selection dramatically impacts your total investment.

### Asphalt Shingles
**Average Total Cost:** $5,500 - $9,500

The most popular roofing material in America, asphalt shingles offer excellent value. Three-tab shingles are the most economical, while architectural (dimensional) shingles cost more but provide better durability and curb appeal.

**Pros:** Affordable, widely available, easy installation
**Cons:** Shorter lifespan (15-30 years)

### Metal Roofing
**Average Total Cost:** $10,000 - $20,000

Metal roofs are gaining popularity due to their longevity and energy efficiency. Options include standing seam, corrugated panels, and metal shingles.

**Pros:** 40-70 year lifespan, energy efficient, low maintenance
**Cons:** Higher upfront cost, potential for noise during rain

### Tile Roofing
**Average Total Cost:** $12,000 - $25,000

Clay and concrete tiles create distinctive, long-lasting roofs popular in Mediterranean and Southwestern architecture.

**Pros:** 50-100 year lifespan, fire resistant, beautiful
**Cons:** Very heavy, requires structural reinforcement, expensive

### Wood Shakes/Shingles
**Average Total Cost:** $8,500 - $16,000

Cedar and other wood roofing materials provide natural beauty and good insulation.

**Pros:** Natural appearance, good insulation
**Cons:** High maintenance, fire risk, shorter lifespan in wet climates

### Slate Roofing
**Average Total Cost:** $18,000 - $45,000

The premium choice for homeowners seeking the ultimate in longevity and elegance.

**Pros:** 75-150 year lifespan, unmatched beauty, fire resistant
**Cons:** Extremely expensive, very heavy, requires specialized installation

## What Factors Affect AC Installation Cost?

### 1. Roof Size and Complexity
Larger roofs require more materials and labor. Complex roofs with multiple valleys, dormers, and angles cost more than simple gable roofs.

### 2. Roof Pitch
Steeper roofs are more dangerous and time-consuming to work on, increasing labor costs. Roofs with pitches over 6/12 often incur pitch premiums of 10-20%.

### 3. Removal and Disposal
Removing old roofing materials typically costs $1-5 per square foot. Multiple layers increase this cost.

### 4. Underlayment and Deck Repair
Quality underlayment (ice and water shield, synthetic underlayment) adds $0.50-2.00 per square foot. Damaged decking requires replacement at $2-5 per square foot.

### 5. Ventilation and Insulation
Proper attic ventilation and insulation upgrades can add $500-2,000 to your project but improve energy efficiency and roof lifespan.

### 6. Location and Accessibility
Urban areas with higher costs of living see higher roofing prices. Difficult roof access increases labor time and costs.

### 7. Permits and Inspections
Most jurisdictions require permits for AC installation, costing $100-500 depending on location.

### 8. Warranty Coverage
Extended manufacturer warranties and workmanship guarantees add upfront cost but provide valuable long-term protection.

# How Much Does AC Repair Cost?

**Quick Answer:** Roof repair costs range from $150 for minor fixes to $3,000+ for major repairs, with most homeowners paying $400-900 for common repairs. Simple shingle replacement costs $150-400, while flashing repairs run $200-500. The exact cost depends on the type and extent of damage, roofing material, and roof accessibility. [Brand Name] offers free HVAC maintenance to provide accurate repair estimates.

Understanding AC repair costs helps you budget for maintenance and decide whether repair or replacement makes more financial sense.

## Average AC Repair Costs by Type

### Minor Repairs ($150-$400)
- **Replacing damaged shingles:** $150-350
- **Sealing small leaks:** $100-250
- **Re-securing loose shingles:** $100-300
- **Minor flashing adjustment:** $150-300

### Moderate Repairs ($400-$1,200)
- **Flashing replacement:** $200-500
- **Valley repair:** $300-600
- **Vent boot replacement:** $200-400
- **Chimney flashing repair:** $300-800
- **Skylight seal repair:** $300-600
- **Fixing small sections (2-3 squares):** $400-900

### Major Repairs ($1,200-$3,000+)
- **Structural deck repair:** $500-2,000
- **Extensive leak repair:** $800-2,500
- **Large section replacement (5+ squares):** $1,500-3,000
- **Sagging AC repair:** $800-2,500
- **Multiple problem areas:** $1,500-4,000

### Emergency Repairs ($300-$1,500)
Storm damage requiring immediate attention often includes emergency service fees of $100-300 on top of standard repair costs.

## AC Repair Cost by Material

Different roofing materials have varying repair costs due to material prices and specialized labor requirements.

### Asphalt Shingle Repair
**Average Cost:** $150-600

The most common and affordable AC repair. Shingles are readily available and most roofers are experienced with repairs.

**Common repairs:**
- Replacing wind-damaged shingles: $150-350
- Fixing curling/lifting shingles: $200-450
- Sealing granule loss areas: $100-300

### Metal AC Repair
**Average Cost:** $300-1,200

Metal roofing repairs require specialized skills and tools.

**Common repairs:**
- Sealing panel seams: $200-500
- Replacing damaged panels: $400-900
- Fixing fastener issues: $300-700

### Tile AC Repair
**Average Cost:** $400-1,500

Tile repairs are delicate work requiring specialized expertise.

**Common repairs:**
- Replacing broken tiles: $400-1,000
- Resealing underlayment: $500-1,200
- Valley repairs: $600-1,500

### Flat AC Repair
**Average Cost:** $300-1,000

Flat roofs (EPDM, TPO, modified bitumen) require different repair techniques.

**Common repairs:**
- Patching membrane: $200-600
- Sealing seams: $300-700
- Drain repair: $250-500

## What Factors Affect AC Repair Costs?

### 1. Extent of Damage
Minor cosmetic damage costs far less than structural issues requiring deck replacement. A thorough inspection identifies all problem areas.

### 2. Roof Accessibility
Steep roofs, multi-story homes, and areas with limited access increase labor costs by 15-30% due to safety equipment and time requirements.

### 3. Roofing Material
Premium materials like slate or tile cost significantly more to repair than standard asphalt shingles due to material costs and specialized labor.

### 4. Roof Age
Older roofs may have brittle or discontinued materials that are harder to match and repair, potentially increasing costs.

### 5. Hidden Damage
Water intrusion often causes unseen damage to underlayment, decking, or insulation, discovered only during repair work.

### 6. Location and Labor Costs
Geographic areas with higher costs of living see higher roofing labor rates, typically $45-75 per hour for skilled roofers.

### 7. Time of Year
Peak season (spring/summer) demand may result in higher prices, while off-season repairs might offer better rates.

### 8. Permit Requirements
Some jurisdictions require permits even for repairs, adding $50-200 to costs.

## Common Roof Problems and Repair Costs

### Roof Leaks
**Cost:** $200-1,500

The most common roofing problem. Simple leaks from damaged shingles cost less; complex leaks requiring extensive investigation cost more.

### Missing or Damaged Shingles
**Cost:** $150-450

Wind, hail, and age cause shingle damage. Replacing a few shingles is affordable, but widespread damage suggests replacement.

### Flashing Failures
**Cost:** $200-800

Flashing around chimneys, vents, and valleys deteriorates over time. Proper flashing repair prevents major water damage.

### Gutter Problems
**Cost:** $150-800

While not technically AC repairs, gutter issues cause roof damage. Cleaning and minor repairs are inexpensive; replacement costs more.

### Pooling Water (Flat Roofs)
**Cost:** $300-1,200

Standing water indicates drainage issues or structural sagging requiring prompt attention to prevent membrane failure.

### Ice Dam Damage
**Cost:** $400-2,000

Winter ice dams force water under shingles. Repairs address both damage and underlying ventilation/insulation issues.

### Emergency HVAC
**Cost:** $500-3,000+

Hail, wind, and falling debris cause varying damage levels. Insurance may cover emergency HVAC repairs.

## AC Repair vs. Replacement: Which Makes Sense?

### Choose Repair When:
- Roof is less than 15 years old
- Damage is localized to one area
- Total repair cost is under 30% of replacement cost
- Roof has 10+ years of useful life remaining
- Damage is from recent isolated incident

### Choose Replacement When:
- Roof is 20+ years old
- Multiple areas need repair
- Repair costs exceed 40% of replacement cost
- Frequent recurring leaks
- Widespread wear throughout the roof

**Rule of thumb:** If repairs cost more than one-third of replacement, replacement is usually the better investment.

Don't wait for a leak. Watch for these indicators:

- Granules in gutters (shingle deterioration)
- Daylight visible through roof boards
- Stains on interior ceilings or walls
- Sagging roof deck
- Damaged or missing flashing
- Moss or algae growth
- Increased energy bills

# How Much Does a Metal Roof Cost?

**Quick Answer:** A metal roof costs $10,000 to $25,000 for an average home, or $6 to $14 per square foot installed. Standing seam metal roofing averages $9-14 per square foot, while corrugated metal panels cost $6-10 per square foot. Though more expensive upfront than asphalt shingles, metal roofs last 40-70 years and offer superior energy efficiency. [Brand Name] provides free metal roofing estimates with multiple material options.

If you're considering a metal roof for your home, understanding the cost breakdown helps you determine whether this premium roofing option fits your budget and goals.

## Average Metal Roof Costs

### By Home Size
- **1,000 sq ft home:** $6,000 - $14,000
- **1,500 sq ft home:** $9,000 - $21,000
- **2,000 sq ft home:** $12,000 - $28,000
- **2,500 sq ft home:** $15,000 - $35,000
- **3,000 sq ft home:** $18,000 - $42,000

### By Material Type
Metal roofing comes in various materials, each with different price points and characteristics.

## Types of Metal Roofing and Costs

### Standing Seam Metal Roofing
**Cost:** $9-14 per square foot installed

The premium choice for residential metal roofing, standing seam features concealed fasteners and vertical panels with raised seams.

**Characteristics:**
- Sleek, modern appearance
- Superior weather protection
- No exposed fasteners
- 40-50 year lifespan
- Best for pitches 3/12 or greater

**Best for:** Homeowners seeking maximum durability and modern aesthetics

### Corrugated Metal Panels
**Cost:** $6-10 per square foot installed

Traditional wavy panels, often seen on agricultural buildings but increasingly popular for residential use.

**Characteristics:**
- Exposed fastener system
- Economical option
- Rustic or industrial aesthetic
- 30-45 year lifespan
- Works on low-slope roofs

**Best for:** Budget-conscious homeowners, modern farmhouse styles

### Metal Shingles/Tiles
**Cost:** $8-13 per square foot installed

Metal formed to mimic traditional roofing materials like wood shakes, slate, or clay tiles.

**Characteristics:**
- Traditional appearance with metal benefits
- Lighter than authentic materials
- Good fire resistance
- 40-60 year lifespan
- More complex installation

**Best for:** Historic homes, HOA restrictions, traditional aesthetics

### Metal Tiles
**Cost:** $10-16 per square foot installed

Individual interlocking metal panels that create a tile appearance.

**Characteristics:**
- Mediterranean or Spanish style
- Lightweight compared to clay
- Excellent wind resistance
- 50-70 year lifespan
- Premium pricing

**Best for:** Southwestern or Mediterranean architecture

## Metal Roofing Cost by Metal Type

The metal composition significantly affects pricing and performance.

### Steel Roofing
**Cost:** $6-12 per square foot

The most common metal roofing material, steel offers excellent strength-to-weight ratio.

**Types:**
- **Galvanized steel:** $6-9 per sq ft (zinc coating)
- **Galvalume steel:** $7-10 per sq ft (aluminum-zinc coating)
- **Painted steel:** $8-12 per sq ft (added color coating)

**Pros:** Strong, affordable, widely available
**Cons:** Can rust if coating is damaged, heavier than aluminum

### Aluminum Roofing
**Cost:** $9-14 per square foot

Excellent for coastal areas due to superior corrosion resistance.

**Pros:** Won't rust, lightweight, salt-air resistant
**Cons:** Softer metal, can dent easier, higher cost

### Copper Roofing
**Cost:** $15-30 per square foot

The premium metal roofing choice with distinctive appearance.

**Pros:** 70+ year lifespan, develops beautiful patina, prestige appearance
**Cons:** Very expensive, special skills required, can stain

### Zinc Roofing
**Cost:** $12-20 per square foot

Growing in popularity, zinc offers unique characteristics.

**Pros:** Self-healing patina, 60-100 year lifespan, eco-friendly
**Cons:** Expensive, limited installer availability

## What's Included in Metal Roof Installation Cost?

### Material Costs (40-50% of total)
- Metal panels or shingles
- Underlayment (synthetic or felt)
- Fasteners and clips
- Trim and flashing
- Ridge caps and closures

### Labor Costs (40-50% of total)
Metal roofing requires specialized skills, commanding higher labor rates than asphalt shingle installation.

- Removal of old roofing: $1-3 per sq ft
- Installation labor: $3-7 per sq ft
- Complex details (valleys, penetrations): additional

### Additional Costs (10-20% of total)
- Deck repairs or replacement
- Upgraded ventilation
- Insulation improvements
- Permits and inspections
- Disposal fees

## Factors Affecting Metal Roof Cost

### 1. Roof Complexity
Simple gable roofs cost significantly less than complex roofs with multiple valleys, dormers, and angles. Complex roofs require more custom fabrication and skilled labor.

### 2. Roof Pitch
Metal roofing works on various pitches, but steeper roofs increase labor costs due to safety requirements and difficulty.

### 3. Roof Size
Larger projects benefit from economies of scale, with per-square-foot costs decreasing slightly on bigger roofs.

### 4. Panel Style and Gauge
Thicker gauge metal (lower numbers like 24-gauge vs. 29-gauge) costs more but offers better durability. Premium panel profiles add cost.

### 5. Color and Finish
Standard colors are most affordable. Premium colors, textured finishes, or custom colors add 10-30% to material costs.

### 6. Geographic Location
Labor and material costs vary by region. Urban areas and regions with higher living costs see higher installation prices.

### 7. Insulation and Underlayment
Quality underlayment ($0.50-2 per sq ft) and insulation upgrades improve performance but increase upfront costs.

### 8. Warranty Level
Extended manufacturer warranties (30-50 years) and premium paint warranties add to material costs.
