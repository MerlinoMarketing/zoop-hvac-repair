# Emergency AC Breakdown Guide for Pompano Beach

When your air conditioning fails in Pompano Beach, it is more than an inconvenience -- it can be a genuine health risk. With summer temperatures regularly exceeding 90 degrees and humidity levels above 70 percent, a home without functioning AC can become dangerously hot within hours. This guide walks you through exactly what to do when your cooling system stops working unexpectedly.

## Immediate Steps When Your AC Fails

Stay calm and work through these steps systematically:

**Step 1: Check the Thermostat.** Verify that the thermostat is set to cool mode and the temperature is set below the current room temperature. Replace the batteries if your thermostat uses them. Sometimes the issue is as simple as a child adjusting the settings or dead batteries.

**Step 2: Check the Circuit Breaker.** Go to your electrical panel and look for the breakers labeled for your AC system -- there may be two, one for the indoor unit and one for the outdoor unit. If either has tripped, switch it fully to the off position, wait 30 seconds, then switch it back on. If the breaker trips again immediately, do not reset it again. This indicates an electrical problem that requires professional attention.

**Step 3: Check the Air Filter.** A severely clogged filter can cause the system to shut down by triggering a safety switch. If the filter is visibly dirty, replace it and restart the system.

**Step 4: Inspect the Outdoor Unit.** Go outside and check whether the condenser unit is running. If the fan is not spinning, check for any visible debris blocking the unit. Listen for any unusual sounds like clicking or humming, which can help a technician diagnose the problem.

**Step 5: Check the Float Switch.** Many Pompano Beach homes have a float switch on the condensate drain line that shuts down the system when the drain is clogged and water backs up. If you see standing water in the drain pan, the float switch may have been triggered. Clearing the clog may restore operation.

## Temporary Cooling Strategies

While waiting for emergency repair service, use these strategies to stay comfortable and safe:

**Close blinds and curtains** on all sun-facing windows to reduce solar heat gain. South-facing and west-facing windows contribute the most heat in Pompano Beach homes.

**Use portable fans and ceiling fans** to circulate air. Even though fans do not lower the temperature, moving air makes the body feel four to six degrees cooler through evaporative cooling.

**Stay hydrated.** Drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol and caffeine, which contribute to dehydration. This is especially important for children, elderly residents, and pets.

**Move to the lowest level of your home.** Heat rises, so the ground floor or any below-grade spaces will be the coolest areas.

**Open windows strategically at night.** If the outside temperature drops below the indoor temperature after sunset, open windows on opposite sides of the house to create cross-ventilation.

**Avoid using heat-generating appliances.** Do not use the oven, dryer, or dishwasher during the hottest parts of the day. Opt for cold meals or outdoor grilling instead.

## When It Becomes a Health Emergency

Certain situations require immediate action beyond AC repair. Be aware of the signs of heat exhaustion and heat stroke, especially in vulnerable household members:

- Heavy sweating followed by a sudden stop in sweating
- Body temperature above 103 degrees
- Confusion, dizziness, or nausea
- Rapid pulse or headache
- Hot, red, dry skin

If anyone in your household shows these symptoms, call 911 immediately, move them to the coolest area available, and apply cool wet cloths to their skin.

## What to Tell the Emergency HVAC Technician

When you call for emergency service, having the following information ready will help the technician prepare and potentially speed up the repair:

- The make and model of your AC system (found on the nameplate of the outdoor unit)
- When the system stopped working and what symptoms you noticed
- Any unusual sounds, smells, or events before the failure
- Whether the system was recently serviced or repaired
- The approximate age of the system

## Preventing Future AC Emergencies

Most emergency breakdowns can be prevented with proper maintenance. Schedule biannual professional tune-ups, replace your air filter monthly, keep the condensate drain line clear, and address minor issues before they become major failures. In the Pompano Beach climate, where your system runs nearly year-round, preventive maintenance is the single most effective way to avoid unexpected breakdowns.
