## Common AC Problems We Fix

### AC Not Cooling
If your air conditioner is running but not producing cold air, the issue could be low refrigerant, a dirty evaporator coil, a failing compressor, or a faulty expansion valve. We use electronic leak detectors and pressure testing to identify the root cause quickly.

### AC Not Turning On
A unit that won't start may have a tripped breaker, a blown capacitor, a faulty contactor, or a bad thermostat connection. Our technicians systematically test each component to pinpoint the failure.

### Strange Noises
Grinding, squealing, rattling, or banging sounds often indicate a worn bearing, loose fan blade, failing motor, or debris in the unit. Ignoring these sounds can lead to more expensive repairs down the line.

### Water Leaks
Water pooling around your indoor unit typically means a clogged condensate drain line, a cracked drain pan, or a frozen evaporator coil. We clear blockages, replace damaged parts, and check for underlying causes.

### Short Cycling
If your AC turns on and off frequently without completing a full cooling cycle, it could be oversized equipment, a dirty air filter, low refrigerant, or an electrical issue. Short cycling increases energy bills and accelerates wear on components.

### High Energy Bills
A sudden spike in your electric bill often points to a struggling AC system. Dirty coils, low refrigerant, duct leaks, and worn components all force your system to work harder and consume more energy.
