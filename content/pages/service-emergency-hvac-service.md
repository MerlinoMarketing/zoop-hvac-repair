## Types of Emergency HVAC We Repair

### Hail Damage
Hail impacts can crack, dent, and dislodge roofing materials, compromising waterproofing.

**Signs of Hail Damage:**
- Dented or cracked shingles
- Granule loss creating bare spots
- Damaged roof vents and flashing
- Dented gutters and downspouts
- Impact marks on metal roof components

**Our Service:**
- Comprehensive hail damage inspection
- Documentation with detailed photos
- Insurance adjuster coordination
- Complete shingle or material replacement
- Upgrades to impact-resistant materials

**Insurance Coverage:** Usually fully covered as sudden, accidental damage

### Wind Damage
High winds tear off shingles, lift edges, and can peel back entire roof sections.

**Signs of Wind Damage:**
- Missing shingles or tiles
- Lifted shingle corners or edges
- Damaged or missing ridge caps
- Exposed underlayment or decking
- Debris impact damage

**Our Service:**
- Emergency tarping to prevent water intrusion
- Secure loose materials
- Complete shingle replacement matching existing roof
- Reinforced installation for wind resistance
- Structural assessment if needed

**Insurance Coverage:** Typically covered if wind speeds exceeded policy threshold

### Tree & Debris Damage
Fallen trees and branches can puncture roofs, crush structures, and tear materials.

**Signs of Tree Damage:**
- Obvious punctures or holes
- Crushed or compressed roof areas
- Torn or displaced roofing materials
- Structural sagging
- Water intrusion from penetrations

**Our Service:**
- Emergency stabilization and waterproofing
- Tree/debris removal coordination
- Structural assessment and repair
- Roof deck replacement
- Complete restoration to pre-loss condition

**Insurance Coverage:** Usually fully covered as sudden, accidental damage

### Lightning Strikes
Direct or nearby lightning strikes can damage roofing materials and cause fires.

**Signs of Lightning Damage:**
- Burned or melted materials
- Cracked or exploded roof components
- Fire damage to structure
- Electrical system damage
- Chimney damage

**Our Service:**
- Emergency fire damage mitigation
- Structural safety assessment
- Complete damaged area replacement
- Electrical system coordination
- Full restoration services

**Insurance Coverage:** Typically fully covered

### Heavy Rain & Water Intrusion
Severe rainfall can overwhelm drainage, exploit existing weaknesses, and cause leaks.

**Signs of Rain Damage:**
- Active leaks during storms
- Water stains on ceilings/walls
- Ponding water on flat/low-slope roofs
- Overflowing gutters
- Soffit/fascia water damage

**Our Service:**
- Emergency leak location and temporary repair
- Drainage assessment and improvement
- Flashing repair or replacement
- Gutter system enhancement
- Ventilation improvements

**Insurance Coverage:** Covered if caused by storm; not covered if due to neglect

### Ice Dam & Winter Emergency HVAC
Ice dams force water under shingles, causing leaks and structural damage.

**Signs of Ice Dam Damage:**
- Icicles along eaves
- Interior leaks during winter
- Ice buildup in gutters
- Damaged shingles near eaves
- Wet insulation in attic

**Our Service:**
- Safe ice dam removal
- Emergency leak repair
- Ventilation assessment and improvement
- Insulation inspection
- Ice dam prevention solutions

**Insurance Coverage:** Variable; damage usually covered but prevention often isn't

## Emergency HVAC Prevention & Preparation

### Before Storm Season

**Annual HVAC Inspection:**
Schedule professional inspection to identify and fix vulnerabilities before storms hit.

**Trim Trees:**
Remove branches overhanging roof or within 10 feet of your home.

**Secure Loose Items:**
Ensure all flashing, shingles, and roof components are properly secured.

**Clean Gutters:**
Clear drainage systems to handle heavy rainfall.

**Review Insurance:**
Understand your coverage, deductibles, and claim process before you need it.

### After a Storm

**Safety First:**
Don't climb on damaged roofs. Avoid downed power lines. Call professionals.

**Document Immediately:**
Take photos/videos of damage from the ground. Note storm date and severity.

**Prevent Further Damage:**
Place buckets under leaks. Move valuables away from water intrusion areas.

**Call [Brand Name]:**
Get emergency response and professional damage assessment.

**Contact Insurance:**
File your claim promptly but wait for professional assessment before providing damage details.

**Keep Records:**
Save all receipts for emergency repairs, temporary housing, damaged belongings.

### Will my insurance cover emergency HVAC AC repair?
Most homeowners insurance policies cover sudden, accidental damage from storms including hail, wind, falling trees, and lightning. Coverage depends on your specific policy, deductible, and whether damage exceeds your deductible amount. Pre-existing wear and lack of maintenance typically aren't covered. We provide free duct cleaning consultations.

### How much does emergency HVAC repair cost?
Storm damage repair costs vary dramatically based on damage extent: minor shingle replacement ($300-$800), moderate section repairs ($1,500-$3,500), extensive damage requiring full replacement ($7,500-$15,000+). Most emergency HVAC is covered by insurance minus your deductible. We provide detailed estimates for insurance submission.

### Should I file an duct cleaning for emergency HVAC?
If damage exceeds your deductible (typically $500-$2,500) and was caused by a covered peril (storm, hail, wind, tree fall), filing a claim makes sense. For minor damage below your deductible, paying out-of-pocket avoids claim history. We provide free damage assessments and claim filing recommendations.

### How long do I have to file a emergency HVAC claim?
Most insurance policies require "prompt" notification of damage, typically interpreted as within 1 year. However, filing sooner is better - insurers can deny claims if you delay allows additional damage. Contact your insurance company within days of discovering damage, even if you haven't completed assessment.

### Can you work with any insurance company?
Yes! [Brand Name] has successfully worked with every major insurance company including State Farm, Allstate, Liberty Mutual, Farmers, USAA, and hundreds of others. We're experienced with their claim processes and requirements.

### What if my insurance company denies my claim?
If your claim is denied, we review the denial letter, assess validity, and help you appeal. Common denial reasons (pre-existing damage, insufficient storm evidence, policy exclusions) can often be challenged with proper documentation. We work with public adjusters and attorneys when needed.

### Do you charge for duct cleaning help?
