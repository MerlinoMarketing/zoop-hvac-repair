## Common Emergency AC Issues

### Capacitor Failure
Capacitors are one of the most common failure points, especially during heat waves when the system runs continuously. A blown capacitor will prevent the compressor or fan from starting.

### Compressor Lockout
When the compressor overheats, it triggers a safety lockout. This can be caused by low refrigerant, electrical issues, or a failing compressor. Our technicians test and diagnose the root cause.

### Refrigerant Leak
A sudden loss of cooling often indicates a refrigerant leak. We use electronic leak detection to find the source, repair the leak, and recharge the system to manufacturer specifications.

### Frozen Evaporator Coil
A frozen coil blocks airflow completely. Common causes include restricted airflow (dirty filter), low refrigerant, or a malfunctioning blower. We thaw the coil and address the underlying problem.

### Electrical Failures
Tripped breakers, blown fuses, and wiring issues can shut down your entire system. We safely diagnose and repair electrical problems to restore operation.
