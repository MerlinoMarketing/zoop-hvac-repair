## AC Installation Cost Factors

### Home Size
Larger roofs require more materials and labor. The average home (2,000 sq ft) with 17-20 squares of roofing typically costs $8,500-$13,000 for asphalt shingles.

### Roof Pitch/Slope
Steeper roofs require more safety equipment and labor time, increasing costs by 10-20%.

### Roof Complexity
Multiple valleys, dormers, skylights, and chimneys increase material waste and labor time.

### Material Selection
Material costs vary dramatically: asphalt shingles ($350-$550/square), metal ($600-$1,200/square), tile ($700-$1,500/square).

### Removal & Disposal
Removing multiple old roof layers or heavy materials like tile adds $100-$200 per square.

### Deck Repair
Rotted or damaged decking requires replacement at $50-$100 per sheet.

### Ventilation Upgrades
Adding or improving ridge vents, soffit vents, or attic fans improves performance but adds cost.

### Location & Accessibility
Difficult access, multi-story homes, or protective requirements (landscaping, pools) increase labor costs.

## AC Installation vs. Roof-Over (Re-Roof)

### Roof-Over: Installing New Shingles Over Old
**Pros:**
- 30-40% less expensive
- Faster installation (1-2 days)
- Less disruption

**Cons:**
- Doesn't address deck problems
- Adds weight to structure
- Voids most manufacturer warranties
- Shorter lifespan than tear-off
- Many codes now prohibit it
- Harder to remove later (double materials)

**Our Recommendation:** We rarely recommend roof-overs except in specific budget-constrained situations. The initial savings often lead to earlier replacement needs and higher total costs.

### Complete Tear-Off & Replacement
**Pros:**
- Identifies and fixes deck problems
- Full manufacturer warranty eligibility
- Maximum lifespan
- Code compliant
- Lighter weight on structure
- Better long-term value

**Cons:**
- Higher initial cost
- Longer installation (2-4 days)
- More disruption during install

**Our Recommendation:** Complete tear-off delivers better long-term value and protection in 95% of cases.
