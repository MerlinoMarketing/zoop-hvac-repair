## When to Replace Your AC System

- **Age**: Most AC systems last 10-15 years in South Florida's demanding climate. If yours is approaching that range, replacement is often more cost-effective than repeated repairs.
- **Frequent Repairs**: If you're calling for service multiple times per year, those repair costs add up quickly.
- **Rising Energy Bills**: Older systems lose efficiency over time. A new high-SEER unit can reduce cooling costs by 30-50%.
- **R-22 Refrigerant**: If your system uses R-22 (Freon), the refrigerant has been phased out and is extremely expensive. Upgrading to an R-410A system saves money long-term.
- **Uneven Cooling**: Hot spots, humidity issues, and temperature inconsistencies often indicate a system that's undersized or failing.

## Our Installation Process

### 1. In-Home Consultation
We visit your property to assess your current system, evaluate ductwork conditions, and discuss your comfort goals and budget. This visit is free with no obligation.

### 2. Manual J Load Calculation
We perform a proper Manual J load calculation to determine the exact cooling capacity your home needs. An oversized or undersized system wastes energy and creates comfort problems.

### 3. Equipment Selection
Based on the load calculation, your budget, and your efficiency goals, we recommend the best equipment options. We install all major brands including Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, and Daikin.

### 4. Professional Installation
Our crews follow manufacturer installation guidelines precisely. This includes proper refrigerant line sizing, electrical connections, condensate drainage, and thermostat wiring.

### 5. System Commissioning
After installation, we verify refrigerant charge, measure airflow, test all safety controls, program your thermostat, and walk you through system operation.

## Efficiency Ratings Explained

- **14 SEER** - Minimum efficiency, lowest upfront cost
- **16 SEER** - Good balance of efficiency and value
- **18-20 SEER** - High efficiency, significant energy savings
- **20+ SEER / Variable Speed** - Premium efficiency with the quietest, most consistent comfort

Higher SEER ratings cost more upfront but save substantially on monthly electric bills, especially in South Florida where AC runs 8-10 months per year.
