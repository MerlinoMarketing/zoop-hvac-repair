## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does duct cleaning assistance cost extra?
No! [Brand Name] provides completely free duct cleaning assistance when you choose us for your AC repairs. We don't charge extra to document damage, meet with adjusters, prepare estimates, or negotiate on your behalf. Our fee is the contracted repair work, which is paid by your insurance minus your deductible.

### Will filing a claim raise my insurance rates?
Filing a claim may impact your rates, but the impact varies by insurance company, your claims history, and the claim cause. Most companies don't raise rates for first-time emergency HVAC claims. We help you evaluate whether filing makes financial sense based on your deductible and damage extent.

### How long does the duct cleaning process take?
Most roof duct cleaning are resolved in 4-8 weeks from filing to final payment. Timeline varies based on insurance company responsiveness, damage complexity, and whether supplements are needed. Emergency repairs happen immediately; permanent repairs begin after claim approval.

### What if my insurance denies my claim?
If your claim is denied, we review the denial letter, assess validity, and help you appeal. Common denials (pre-existing damage, maintenance neglect, insufficient evidence) can often be overcome with proper documentation and advocacy. We work with public adjusters and attorneys for complex disputes.

### Do I have to use my insurance company's preferred contractor?
No! You have the legal right to choose any licensed contractor. Insurance companies may recommend contractors, but you're not obligated to use them. Choose a contractor you trust who will advocate for you, not one who works frequently with insurance companies and may be incentivized to minimize costs.

### Should I file a claim for minor damage?
File a claim only if damage exceeds your deductible (typically $500-$2,500) and is from a covered peril. Filing claims for minor damage below your deductible creates claim history without benefit. We provide free assessments to help you decide.

### What is recoverable depreciation?
Insurance companies initially pay "Actual Cash Value" (replacement cost minus depreciation). After repairs are completed, you receive "recoverable depreciation" - the withheld amount. This two-payment system ensures you complete repairs rather than pocketing insurance money.

### Can you attend the adjuster meeting on my behalf?
Yes! We attend all adjuster meetings, point out damage, answer technical questions, and ensure nothing is missed. You're welcome to attend as well, but our presence ensures professional advocacy and proper documentation.
