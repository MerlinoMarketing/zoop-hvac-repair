# Heat Pump Services in Pompano Beach, FL

## Efficient Heating and Cooling in One System

Heat pumps are an increasingly popular choice for South Florida homes because they provide both cooling and heating from a single system. Instead of generating heat like a furnace, a heat pump transfers heat — extracting it from outdoor air and moving it inside during winter, and reversing the process for cooling in summer.

## Why Heat Pumps Make Sense in South Florida

South Florida's mild winters make heat pumps the ideal HVAC solution. Unlike northern climates where temperatures regularly drop below freezing (reducing heat pump efficiency), Pompano Beach's winter lows rarely dip below 50 degrees — well within a heat pump's optimal operating range.

### Benefits for South Florida Homeowners
- **Year-Round Comfort** - One system handles both cooling and the occasional heating need
- **Energy Efficiency** - Heat pumps use 50% less electricity for heating than electric resistance systems
- **Lower Utility Bills** - High SEER ratings deliver excellent cooling efficiency
- **Smaller Footprint** - No separate furnace or heating system needed
- **Environmentally Friendly** - Reduced energy consumption means lower carbon emissions

## Types of Heat Pumps

### Air-Source Heat Pumps
The most common type. These systems extract heat from outdoor air (even in cool weather) and transfer it inside. Modern air-source heat pumps are highly efficient in South Florida's climate.

### Ductless Mini-Split Heat Pumps
Perfect for homes without ductwork, room additions, or supplementing central systems in problem areas. Each indoor unit can be independently controlled for zone-by-zone comfort.

### Dual-Fuel / Hybrid Systems
These systems pair a heat pump with a gas furnace backup. The heat pump handles cooling and mild heating, while the furnace kicks in during rare cold snaps. An option for homes with existing gas lines.

## Common Heat Pump Issues

- **Not Heating** - Often caused by a faulty reversing valve, low refrigerant, or defrost issues
- **Icing Up** - Can indicate restricted airflow, low refrigerant, or defrost board failure
- **Running Constantly** - May be undersized, low on refrigerant, or have a thermostat issue
- **Switching Between Modes** - Typically a reversing valve solenoid or thermostat wiring problem
