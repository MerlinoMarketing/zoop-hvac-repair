# AC Installation Cost Guide for South Florida Homeowners

Replacing or installing a new air conditioning system is one of the most significant home investments you will make in Pompano Beach. Understanding the costs involved helps you budget effectively and avoid overpaying. This guide breaks down what South Florida homeowners can expect to pay for a new AC installation in 2024 and beyond.

## Average AC Installation Costs in Pompano Beach

The total cost of a new AC system installation in the Pompano Beach area typically ranges from $5,000 to $15,000, depending on several factors. Here is a general breakdown by system type:

**Central Air Conditioning (Split System):** $5,500 to $10,000. This is the most common type installed in Pompano Beach homes. It includes an outdoor condenser unit and an indoor air handler connected by refrigerant lines.

**Heat Pump System:** $6,000 to $12,000. Heat pumps are increasingly popular in South Florida because they provide both cooling and heating. While the upfront cost is slightly higher, the energy savings can be significant.

**Ductless Mini-Split System:** $3,000 to $8,000 for a single-zone system, or $10,000 to $20,000 for a multi-zone system. These are ideal for older Pompano Beach homes without existing ductwork or for room additions.

**Package Unit:** $6,000 to $11,000. These self-contained units are common in South Florida homes with limited indoor space, as all components are housed in a single outdoor unit.

## What Affects Installation Cost

Several factors influence the final price of your AC installation:

**Home Size and Cooling Load:** Larger homes require larger systems. In South Florida, a general rule is one ton of cooling capacity per 400 to 500 square feet, though factors like insulation, window count, and sun exposure also matter. An undersized system will run constantly and wear out prematurely, while an oversized system will short-cycle and create humidity problems -- both common issues in Broward County homes.

**SEER Rating:** The Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio measures how efficiently the system operates. As of 2023, the minimum SEER rating for new installations in the Southeast region is 15 SEER (or SEER2 equivalent). Higher-efficiency systems (16 to 20+ SEER) cost more upfront but deliver lower monthly energy bills. In Pompano Beach, where AC accounts for 40 to 60 percent of your electric bill, a higher SEER rating often pays for itself within a few years.

**Ductwork Condition:** If your existing ductwork is damaged, poorly sealed, or undersized, it may need repair or replacement. Ductwork modifications can add $1,500 to $5,000 to the installation cost but are essential for proper system performance.

**Electrical Upgrades:** Older Pompano Beach homes may require electrical panel upgrades to support a new, more powerful AC system. This can add $1,000 to $3,000 to the project.

**Permit and Inspection Fees:** Broward County requires permits for AC installations. Permit costs typically range from $150 to $400. A reputable contractor will handle the permitting process for you.

## Hidden Costs to Watch For

Be aware of potential additional expenses that some contractors may not include in their initial quote:

- Removal and disposal of the old system ($300 to $500)
- Thermostat upgrade ($150 to $500 depending on features)
- Concrete pad for the outdoor unit ($150 to $300)
- Refrigerant line set replacement ($300 to $600)
- Code compliance upgrades for older homes

## Financing Options

Most reputable HVAC companies in Pompano Beach offer financing options to make the investment more manageable. Common options include manufacturer financing with promotional interest rates, home improvement loans through local banks, PACE (Property Assessed Clean Energy) financing available in Broward County, and FPL rebate programs for high-efficiency installations.

## Getting the Best Value

To ensure you get the best value on your AC installation, obtain at least three written quotes from licensed contractors, compare equipment brands and warranty terms rather than just price, ask about available manufacturer rebates and utility incentives, verify that the contractor will pull proper Broward County permits, and request a Manual J load calculation to ensure proper system sizing.

## When to Replace vs. Repair

As a general guideline, consider replacement if your system is more than 10 to 15 years old, repair costs exceed 50 percent of a new system, your system uses R-22 refrigerant (which is being phased out), or your energy bills have been steadily increasing despite maintenance.
