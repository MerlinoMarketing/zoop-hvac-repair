## Common Roof Problems That Need Repair {#common-roof-problems}

Understanding the most common roofing issues helps you identify problems early, before they become expensive disasters. Here are the AC repair issues [Company Name] sees most frequently in [City] homes:

### Missing or Damaged Shingles

**What to Look For:**
- Shingles completely missing from your HVAC system
- Cracked, curled, or cupped shingles
- Shingles with granule loss (appearing bald or discolored)
- Broken or torn shingle edges

**Why It Happens:**
Wind damage is the leading cause of missing shingles, particularly during [City]'s storm season. Age and thermal expansion/contraction cycles also cause shingles to crack and curl over time.

**Repair Urgency:** High. Missing or damaged shingles expose the underlayment and roof deck to moisture, leading to leaks and wood rot.

**Typical Cost:** $150-$400 for spot shingle replacement

### Roof Leaks and Water Damage

**What to Look For:**
- Water stains on ceilings or walls
- Dripping water during or after rain
- Musty odors in the attic
- Mold or mildew growth
- Sagging ceiling areas

**Why It Happens:**
HVAC breakdowns develop from multiple sources: damaged shingles, failed flashing, ice dam damage, clogged gutters causing water backup, or worn roof penetrations around vents and chimneys.

**Repair Urgency:** Critical. Active leaks cause progressive damage to insulation, drywall, framing, and can lead to dangerous mold growth.

**Typical Cost:** $300-$1,000 depending on leak source and extent of damage

**Pro Tip:** Don't wait on HVAC breakdown repair. What starts as a $400 repair can become a $4,000+ problem involving ceiling replacement and mold remediation. For immediate help, contact [Company Name] for {{LINK:emergency-ac-repair|emergency HVAC breakdown repair}} in [City].

### Damaged or Failed Flashing

**What to Look For:**
- Rust or corrosion on metal flashing
- Gaps between flashing and roof surface
- Bent or missing flashing pieces
- Leaks near chimneys, skylights, or roof valleys

**Why It Happens:**
Flashing—the metal strips that seal roof transitions and penetrations—degrades from UV exposure, thermal cycling, and physical damage. Poor initial installation is also common.

**Repair Urgency:** High. Damaged flashing is a primary leak source that quickly leads to interior water damage.

**Typical Cost:** $200-$500 for flashing repairs; $600-$1,200 for chimney reflashing

### Gutter and Drainage Issues

**What to Look For:**
- Sagging or detached gutters
- Water pooling on roof surface
- Ice dams forming in winter
- Overflow during moderate rain

**Why It Happens:**
Clogged gutters from leaves and debris cause water to back up under shingles. Improperly sloped gutters don't drain effectively. Undersized gutters can't handle [City]'s heavy rainfall.

**Repair Urgency:** Medium to High. Poor drainage accelerates roof deterioration and causes fascia rot.

**Typical Cost:** $150-$400 for gutter repairs; $200-$600 for related roof edge repairs

### Roof Ventilation Problems

**What to Look For:**
- Excessive attic heat in summer
- Ice dams in winter
- Moisture or frost in attic
- Premature shingle aging
- Higher energy bills

**Why It Happens:**
Inadequate attic ventilation traps heat and moisture, cooking shingles from below and creating condensation issues. Blocked soffit vents or insufficient ridge ventilation are common culprits.

**Repair Urgency:** Medium. Ventilation issues shorten roof lifespan by years but rarely cause immediate leaks.

**Typical Cost:** $300-$900 for adding or repairing roof vents

### Storm and Impact Damage

**What to Look For:**
- Dents in shingles from hail
- Broken shingles from fallen branches
- Punctures or tears in roofing material
- Damaged or dented flashing and vents

**Why It Happens:**
Severe weather events—hailstorms, high winds, falling trees—cause concentrated damage that may not be immediately obvious but compromises roof integrity.

**Repair Urgency:** High. Storm damage typically qualifies for insurance coverage but must be documented and repaired promptly.

**Typical Cost:** $500-$2,500 depending on extent; often covered by homeowners insurance

## AC Repair vs Replacement: Making the Right Choice {#repair-vs-replacement}

One of the most important decisions homeowners face is whether to repair or replace their roof. Here's how to make that determination:

### Repair Makes Sense When:

**Limited Damage**
- Damage covers less than 30% of roof surface
- Problem is isolated to one area or roof section
- Underlying structure (decking, rafters) is sound

**Newer Roof**
- Roof is less than 15 years old (for asphalt shingles)
- Shingles match current roof in style and color
- Warranty coverage is still active

**Budget Constraints**
- Immediate funds for full replacement aren't available
- Repair solves the pressing problem effectively
- You plan to sell within 3-5 years

**Example Scenario:** A 10-year-old roof with emergency HVAC on one slope totaling $800 to repair versus $12,000 for full replacement. Repair is clearly the right choice.

### Replacement Makes Sense When:

**Extensive Damage**
- Damage covers more than 30% of roof
- Multiple areas show significant wear
- Structural damage to decking exists

**Aging Roof**
- Roof is beyond 75% of expected lifespan
- Widespread shingle deterioration
- Previous repairs have failed

**Recurring Problems**
- You've made multiple repairs in past 2-3 years
- New leaks keep developing
- Repair costs are approaching 25%+ of replacement cost

**Long-Term Plans**
- You plan to stay in home 5+ years
- You want to increase home value
- Energy efficiency improvements are priority

**Example Scenario:** A 22-year-old roof with leaks in three locations, widespread granule loss, and $2,500 in estimated repairs. Replacement provides better long-term value and comes with a new warranty.

## When Repair Becomes Replacement {#replacement-threshold}

Sometimes repairs no longer make financial sense, and full replacement is the better investment.

### The Replacement Threshold

**Age-Based Decision:**
- **Under 50% of lifespan** (e.g., 10 years on 20-year shingles): Repair almost always makes sense
- **50-75% of lifespan** (e.g., 15 years on 20-year shingles): Evaluate based on extent and cost
- **Over 75% of lifespan** (e.g., 18+ years on 20-year shingles): Replacement usually better long-term value

**Cost-Based Decision:**
If repair costs exceed these thresholds, consider replacement:
- **25% of replacement cost** on roofs over 15 years old
- **50% of replacement cost** on roofs under 10 years old
- **Multiple repairs totaling 40%+ of replacement cost** within 3 years

**Condition-Based Decision:**
Replacement is likely necessary when:
- More than 30% of roof surface shows damage or wear
- Widespread granule loss and bare spots on shingles
- Multiple leaks in different roof areas
- Sagging roof deck indicating structural issues
- Previous repairs have failed repeatedly

**Scenario 2: Replacement Makes Sense**

**Repair Advantages:**

**Replacement Advantages:**
